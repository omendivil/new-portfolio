Experience Section

Needs its own wow moment. Not code-themed. Physical/tactile.

Experience Option A — "The Stack"
Swipeable card deck you deal through
Three premium cards stacked on top of each other. Swipe or drag the top card off to reveal the next. Each card has its own visual personality. Mobile-native — swiping feels natural on touch. A "1/3" counter tracks position.

Projects Section Enhancements

Building on your existing showcase. These stack together.

Apple-style "zoom into existence" on scroll
As you scroll the projects section into view, the stage doesn't just fade — it zooms up from 88% scale, the device frame materializes inside it 150ms later, then HUD overlays stagger in with their existing cinematic delays. Like watching a product reveal itself.

Project Enhancement 2 — Perspective Tilt + Parallax HUD
The stage tilts toward your cursor with depth-separated layers
Mouse over the project stage and it gently tilts toward your cursor (max ~6°). The HUD overlays move at a different rate than the video — creating real depth. Like picking up a phone to look at it from an angle. On mobile: subtle gyroscope tilt.
The Stage
Outer shell gets perspective: 1200px. Mouse position → rotateX/Y via useSpring. Max ±6°. Springs back to flat on mouse leave.
The Parallax
HUD overlays translate at 1.5x the mouse offset. They float above the video. Creates real depth separation — like UI printed on glass hovering over the screen.
⭐ Must-have. This is what makes Stripe and Apple product pages feel "alive." Visitors will instinctively move their mouse over it just to see the effect. Uses useMotionValue + useSpring — already in your Framer Motion install. The parallax between HUD and video is the detail that separates craft from template.
Project Enhancement 3 — Morphing Stage Transitions
Project switches feel continuous, not binary
When you click a different pill: the stage background color shifts per project (warm amber for iOS, cool blue for AI, neutral for web). HUD text slides out directionally while new text slides in from the opposite side. The device frame smoothly morphs between phone and canvas aspect ratios.

My recommendation for Experience:
The Boarding Pass if you want maximum craft and attention to detail — the holographic tilt, the flip, the barcode, the travel metaphor all tell a story and show obsessive design. It's more build time but it's the one people will share.

The Stack if you want maximum interaction and mobile impact — the swipe gesture is instantly engaging and works better on phones. Faster to build. Less visual detail but more physical feel.

Either way, both are "wowers" that match the diff view hero's energy.
